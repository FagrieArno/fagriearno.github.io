Research Projects

Population Analysis of Fusarium pseudograminearum from South African Wheat

Abstract: This study investigated the genetic diversity and population structure of Fusarium pseudograminearum isolates collected from wheat fields across South Africa. The research aimed to understand the pathogen's distribution patterns and evaluate the effectiveness of cultural management practices in controlling crown rot disease. Using microsatellite markers and phylogenetic analysis, we characterized 150 isolates from major wheat-producing regions. The findings revealed significant genetic diversity within the population, with distinct geographical clustering patterns that suggest limited gene flow between regions. Cultural practices including crop rotation, residue management, and planting date adjustments showed varying degrees of effectiveness in disease suppression.

Integrated Disease Management Strategies for Fungal Pathogens in Agricultural Systems

Abstract: This comprehensive study evaluated the effectiveness of integrated disease management (IDM) approaches for controlling major fungal pathogens in South African agricultural systems. The research combined cultural, biological, and chemical control methods to develop sustainable disease management strategies for smallholder and commercial farmers. Field trials were conducted across multiple seasons and locations, testing various combinations of resistant cultivars, biocontrol agents, fungicide applications, and cultural practices. Economic analysis was performed to assess the cost-effectiveness of different management strategies. The study demonstrated that integrated approaches consistently outperformed single-method strategies, reducing disease incidence by 60-80% while maintaining economic viability. The research provided evidence-based recommendations for sustainable disease management that balance effectiveness, environmental impact, and economic considerations.

RT-PCR Analysis of Defense-Related Genes in Maize Resistance to Fusarium verticillioides

Abstract: This research focused on understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying maize resistance to Fusarium verticillioides, the causal agent of ear rot and stalk rot diseases. Using quantitative RT-PCR, we analyzed the expression patterns of key defense-related genes including pathogenesis-related proteins, phytoalexin biosynthesis enzymes, and transcription factors in resistant and susceptible maize cultivars following pathogen inoculation. The study involved controlled greenhouse experiments with multiple time-point sampling to capture the temporal dynamics of gene expression. Results demonstrated differential expression of 15 defense genes, with resistant cultivars showing rapid and sustained upregulation of antimicrobial compounds and cell wall reinforcement genes. This work contributes to the development of molecular markers for resistance breeding programs.
